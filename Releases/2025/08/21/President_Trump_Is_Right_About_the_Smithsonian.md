# President Trump Is Right About the Smithsonian

- The National Museum of African American History and Culture [debuted a series](https://archive.ph/Y6NLt) to educate people on “a society that privileges white people and whiteness” — [defining](https://www.theamericanconservative.com/smithsonian-whiteness-anti-white-propaganda/) so-called “ [white dominant culture](https://x.com/RapidResponse47/status/1958252901685772373) “ as “ways white people and their traditions, attitudes, and ways of life have been normalized over time” and [portraying](https://www.aei.org/op-eds/smithsonian-institution-explains-that-rationality-hard-work-are-racist/) “the nuclear family,” “work ethic,” and “intellect” as white qualities rooted in racism.

  - The campaign featured [content](https://archive.ph/Y6NLt) from [hardcore woke activist](https://thefederalist.com/2020/08/14/the-woke-war-on-history-how-ibram-x-kendi-and-others-distort-facts-to-fit-narratives/) Ibram X. Kendi.

- As part of its [campaign](https://www.nytimes.com/2022/04/27/arts/design/national-portrait-gallery-performances.html) to stop being “wealthy, pale, and male,” the National Portrait Gallery featured a choreographed “ [modern dance performance](https://www.si.edu/events/detail?trumbaEmbed=view%3Devent%26eventid%3D158636545) “ detailing the “ [ramifications](https://npg.si.edu/about-us/press-release/dana-tai-soon-burgess-dance-company-debut-two-new-performances-its-national) “ of the southern border wall and [commissioned an entire series](https://npg.si.edu/exhibit/identify/index.html) to examine “American portraiture and institutional history… through the lens of historical exclusion.”

- The American History Museum prominently displays the “Intersex-Inclusive Progress Pride flag” [at its entrance](https://thefederalist.com/2025/08/15/smithsonians-american-history-museum-is-wall-to-wall-anti-american-propaganda/#:~:text=At%20the%20American%20History%20Museum%2C%20which%20is%20located%20in%20the%20shadow%20of%20the%20Washington%20Monument%2C%20Americans%20who%20want%20to%20learn%20about%20their%20history%20must%20first%20be%20bombarded%20at%20the%20front%20entrance%20with%20a%20%E2%80%9Cpride%2Dprogress%E2%80%9D%20flag.), which was also [flown](https://www.cooperhewitt.org/2023/06/20/intersex-inclusive-progress-pride-flag-at-the-smithsonian/) alongside the American flag at multiple Smithsonian campuses.

- The National Portrait Gallery [features](https://portraitcompetition.si.edu/exhibition/2022-outwin-boochever-portrait-competition/refugees-crossing-the-border-wall-into-south-texas/) art commemorating the act of illegally crossing the “inclusive and exclusionary” southern border — even making it a finalist for one of its awards.

- The National Museum of African Art [displayed](https://www.si.edu/newsdesk/releases/deep-wake-drexciya-ayana-v-jackson-opens-april-29-national-museum-african-art) an exhibit on “works of speculative fiction that bring to life an immersive, feminist and sacred aquatopia inspired by the legend of Drexciya,” an “underwater kingdom populated by the children of pregnant women who had been thrown overboard or jumped into the ocean during the Middle Passage.”

- The American History Museum’s “LGBTQ+ History” exhibit [seeks](https://americanhistory.si.edu/explore/topics/lgbtq-history#:~:text=The%20museum%20is,many%20other%20experiences.) to “understand evolving and overlapping identities such as lesbian, gay, bi-sexual, transgender, queer, transsexual, transvestite, mahu, homosexual, fluid, invert, urning, third sex, two sex, gender-bender, sapphist, hijra, friend of Dorothy, drag queen/king, and many other experiences,” and includes articles on “ [LGBTQ+ inclusion and skateboarding](https://americanhistory.si.edu/explore/stories/place-park-lgbtq-inclusion-and-skateboarding) “ and “ [the rise of drag ball culture in the 1920s](https://americanhistory.si.edu/explore/stories/queens-and-queers-rise-drag-ball-culture-1920s).”

- The National Museum of the American Latino features [programming](https://latino.si.edu/exhibitions/GAC/thriving-diversity) highlighting “animated Latinos and Latinas with disabilities” — with [content](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dlp0S0UAA2o&t=20s) from “a disabled, plus-sized actress” and an “ambulatory wheelchair user” who “educates on their identity being Latinx, LGBTQ+, and disabled.”

- The National Museum of the American Latino [characterizes](https://thefederalist.com/2025/08/15/smithsonians-american-history-museum-is-wall-to-wall-anti-american-propaganda/#:~:text=The%20Texas%20Revolution%2C%20according%20to%20the%20exhibit%2C%20was%20actually%20a%20massive%20defense%20of%20slavery%20waged%20by%20%E2%80%9Cwhite%20Anglo%20Saxon%E2%80%9D%20settlers%20against%20anti%2Dslavery%20Mexicans%20fighting%20for%20freedom%2C%20not%20a%20Texan%20war%20of%20independence%20from%20Mexico.) the Texas Revolution as a “massive defense of slavery waged by ‘white Anglo Saxon’ settlers against anti-slavery Mexicans fighting for freedom, not a Texan war of independence from Mexico,” and [frames](https://latino.si.edu/exhibitions/presente/historical-legacies/1800s-printing-press) the Mexican-American War as “the North American invasion” that was “unprovoked and motivated by pro-slavery politicians.”

- According to the National Museum of the American Latino, “ [what unites Latinas and Latinos](https://latino.si.edu/exhibitions/presente/latino-identity#:~:text=What%20unites%20Latinas%20and%20Latinos%3F%C2%A0) “ is “the Black Lives Matter movement.”

- The National Portrait Gallery [commissioned](https://portraitcompetition.si.edu/competition/commissioned-works/) a “stop-motion drawing animation” that “ [examines the career](https://portraitcompetition.si.edu/competition/commissioned-works/portrait-dr-anthony-fauci/) “ of Anthony Fauci.

- The American History Museum’s exhibit marking the 50 th anniversary of Title IX includes biological men competing in women’s sports and [argues in favor](https://thefederalist.com/2025/08/15/smithsonians-american-history-museum-is-wall-to-wall-anti-american-propaganda/#:~:text=True%20to%20form,traditional%20gender%20norms.%E2%80%9D) of “transgender” athletes competing in sports against the opposite biological sex.

- A exhibit at the American History Museum [depicts](https://thefederalist.com/2025/08/15/smithsonians-american-history-museum-is-wall-to-wall-anti-american-propaganda/#:~:text=To%20further%20that,non%2DWhite%20immigration.%E2%80%9D) migrants watching Independence Day fireworks “through an opening in the U.S.-Mexico border wall” and says America’s founders “feared non-White immigration.”

- The American History Museum features a display that [refers](https://thefederalist.com/2025/08/15/smithsonians-american-history-museum-is-wall-to-wall-anti-american-propaganda/#:~:text=The%20founding%20of%20America%2C%20from%20the%20colonial%20era%20through%20the%20revolution%2C%20was%20not%20a%20historic%20triumph%20of%20liberty%2C%20the%20American%20History%20Museum%20insists%2C%20but%20rather%20a%20%E2%80%9Cprofound%20unsettling%20of%20the%20continent.%E2%80%9D) to the founding of America as “a profound unsettling of the continent.”

- The American History Museum’s “American Democracy” exhibit [claims](https://americanhistory.si.edu/explore/exhibitions/american-democracy/online/vote-voice/keeping-vote) voter integrity measures are “attempts to minimize the political power” of “new and diverse groups of Americans,” while its [section](https://americanhistory.si.edu/explore/exhibitions/american-democracy/online/beyond-ballot/petitioning-your-feet) on “demonstrations” includes only leftist causes.

- An American History Museum [exhibit](https://thefederalist.com/2025/08/15/smithsonians-american-history-museum-is-wall-to-wall-anti-american-propaganda/) [features](https://americanhistory.si.edu/explore/stories/new-americans#:~:text=The%20Immokalee%20Statue%20of%20Liberty%20is%20holding%20a%20tomato%20in%20her%20right%20hand%20instead%20of%20a%20torch%2C%20and%20a%20basket%20of%20tomatoes%20in%20her%20left%20hand%20instead%20of%20a%20tablet.%20Her%20bronze%20skin%20tone%20represents%20workers%20from%20Mexico%20and%20Central%20America%20who) a depiction of the Statue of Liberty “holding a tomato in her right hand instead of a torch, and a basket of tomatoes in her left hand instead of a tablet.”

- The National Museum of the American Latino [features](https://thefederalist.com/2025/08/15/smithsonians-american-history-museum-is-wall-to-wall-anti-american-propaganda/) an anti-American exhibit that [defines](https://www.nationalreview.com/2023/07/smithsonian-peddles-racism-and-hate-at-the-museum-of-the-american-latino/) Latino history as centuries of victimhood and exploitation, [suggests](https://thefederalist.com/2025/08/15/smithsonians-american-history-museum-is-wall-to-wall-anti-american-propaganda/) the U.S. is stolen land, and [characterizes](https://latino.si.edu/exhibitions/presente/historical-legacies) U.S. history as rooted in “colonization.”

  - The exhibit features [writing](https://latino.si.edu/exhibitions/presente/immigration-stories/jairo-javier-morales-graduation-cap-and-stole) from illegal immigrants “fighting to belong.”

  - The exhibit displays a quote from Claudia de la Cruz, the [socialist](https://votesocialist2024.com/) nominee for president and a director an [anti-American hate group](https://www.aei.org/op-eds/code-pinks-denial-of-the-uyghur-genocide-makes-it-a-hate-group/), as well as [another quote](https://thefederalist.com/2025/08/15/smithsonians-american-history-museum-is-wall-to-wall-anti-american-propaganda/#:~:text=Stand%2Dalone%20quote%20from%20the%20American%20History%20Museum) that reads, “We didn’t cross the border; the border crossed us.”

  - The exhibit remains [prominently featured](https://archive.ph/lXErr) on its website alongside a quote from the Communist Party USA’s Angela Davis, who was once [among](https://www.history.com/articles/angela-davis-fbi-most-wanted-list) the FBI’s Top 10 Most Wanted Fugitives.
